I am a recent graduate of Boston University with a B.A. in Computer Science and a minor in Biology. My academic experience was comprised of a focus in Data Science and Data Engineering. During my time at BU I worked with a variety of real world datasets, from natural language data to spatiotemporal databases. I also became familiar with a multitude of tools and techniques to analyse and index these datasets, including Dimensionality Reduction, Unsupervised Learning, NLP, and Regressions.

The data science curriculum at Boston University nurtured my curiosity for learning new technologies, and also increased my ability to understand the benefits and shortcomings of each attribute in a dataset. This curiosity has allowed me to clearly and creatively explain and produce solutions to a variety of unclear problems.

Prior to deciding to pursue Data Science, I gained experience in Software Development through a variety of personal projects as well as with an internship as a QA analyst at Conduent, a government and business technology company. While at Conduent I assisted in the development of the New Jersey EZ-Pass IVR and website. I frequently participated in meetings with the development team, where I was tasked with clearly communicating problems and potential solutions to both developers and managers.

If you would like to know more about me or my projects, I encourage you to review my resume or contact me via email.
